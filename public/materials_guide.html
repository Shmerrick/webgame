A Crafter's Guide to Materials

Welcome, artisan! This guide unveils the secrets of the materials you'll use to forge legendary weapons and armor. Our world is built on a simple principle: the properties of materials in our world mirror those of the real world, bringing a unique depth to the art of crafting.

The Soul of a Material

Every item you craft carries the essence of its components. We've translated the science of real-world materials into tangible gameplay stats. A heavy, dense metal will feel weighty and powerful. A light, flexible wood will be nimble and quick. This means your choice of material is not just cosmetic—it's a strategic decision that will define your combat style.

From Science to Swordcraft

So, how does a simple rock become a mighty shield? We look at a few key scientific properties:

  • Density: Determines an item's weight. Heavier items hit harder (more Blunt damage) but can be slower to wield.
  • Hardness: A material's ability to resist being scratched or dented. Hard materials excel at Slashing and Piercing.
  • Stiffness (Elastic Modulus): How much a material bends under stress. Stiff materials are great for piercing attacks, while more flexible ones can absorb blunt force.
  • Toughness: A material's resistance to breaking. Tough materials make for durable armor that can take a beating.
  • Conductivity & Resistance: How a material handles heat and electricity. This directly influences its resistance to Fire and Wind magic.

Understanding Your Stats

These properties are distilled into the stats you see on your items. Here’s what they mean for you on the battlefield:

Offensive Stats

Offensive stats determine the type of damage your weapon inflicts.

  • Slash: The ability to cut through soft targets. Ideal for weapons made from hard materials like steel. Effective against lightly armored foes.
  • Pierce: The power to punch through armor. Stiff, hard materials like obsidian and bone make for excellent spearheads and arrowheads.
  • Blunt: The force of impact. Dense, heavy materials like lead and iron are perfect for hammers and maces that crush and stagger opponents.

Defensive Stats

Defensive stats on your armor determine how well you resist different types of attacks.

  • Slash Defense: Reduces damage from cutting attacks. Tough, hard materials that don't tear easily provide the best slash defense.
  • Pierce Defense: Protects against arrows, spears, and daggers. Materials that are both hard and stiff are needed to stop a piercing point.
  • Blunt Defense: Absorbs the shock from heavy blows. Softer, more flexible materials can distribute the force of a blow, preventing broken bones. This is why even heavy plate armor has soft padding underneath!

Elemental Resistances

The world is filled with magic. The right material can save you from a fiery end.

  • Fire Resistance: Materials that don't conduct heat well, like stone and certain minerals, offer protection from flames.
  • Water Resistance: Materials that don't corrode or soak up water will resist corrosive and water-based spells.
  • Wind Resistance: In our world, wind magic is tied to electricity. Materials that are poor electrical conductors will shield you from shocking attacks.
  • Earth Resistance: Dense and tough materials are your best bet for withstanding the crushing force of earth magic.

The Crafter's Progression: Tiers of Power

As you explore the world, you'll discover new and rarer materials, organized into five tiers of quality. While a Tier 1 material can create a serviceable piece of gear, only a master artisan working with legendary Tier 5 components can craft items of myth.

Metals

The backbone of any armory. From the humble copper to the legendary adamantite, metals offer a balance of offense and defense.

  • T1: Iron, Copper, Tin, Lead
  • T2: Bronze, Brass
  • T3: Steels (Carbon, Damascus)
  • T4: Titanium, Tungsten
  • T5: Mithril, Adamantite

Organics

Harvested from the flora and fauna of the world, organic materials are versatile and offer unique properties.

  • Woods: Pine → Oak → Hickory → Lignum Vitae → Worldtree Yew
  • Leathers: Goat Hide → Cowhide → Crocodile Hide → Rhino Hide → Dragonhide
  • Furs: Rabbit Fur → Wolf Fur → Bear Fur → Polar Bear Fur → Phoenix Down
  • Silks: Mulberry Silk → Tussar Silk → Spider Silk → Giant Spider Silk → Arachne Silk
  • Scales: Snake Scale → Fish Scale → Crocodile Scale → Pangolin Scale → Dragon Scale
  • Bones: Chicken Bone → Deer Bone → Cow Bone → Whale Bone → Titanbone

Rocks & Minerals

Dug from the deepest parts of the earth, these materials are often heavy and exceptionally resistant to the elements.

  • Rocks: Sandstone → Limestone → Granite → Basalt → Worldstone
  • Minerals: Quartz → Calcite → Obsidian → Corundum → Aethercrystal

A Note on Weapon Damage

The material you choose for your weapon's head is paramount. It determines the primary damage type and how much it hurts. A sword made with a sharp, hard steel head will deal excellent Slash damage. A hammer with a dense lead head will be a master of Blunt force.

Furthermore, you can imbue your weapons with elemental magic. This adds a new layer of damage on top of the physical damage, allowing you to exploit your enemy's elemental weaknesses. The material of your weapon head also influences how well it holds an enchantment, making some materials better conduits for magic than others.
